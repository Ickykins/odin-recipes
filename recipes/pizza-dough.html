Pizza Dough

Description

Ingredients

  • 6g Honey
  • 4.5g active dry yeast/instant yeast/1 packet active dry yeast
  • 9g table salt
  • 227g warm (but NOT hot) water
  • 12.5g olive oil (optional)
  • 330 - 720g unbleached all purpose flour

Instructions

  1. Dissolve honey, yeast, and olive oil in warm water and let proof for 10 minutes
  2. Add flour and salt in seperate bowl and mix. Make a well in the flour and pour yeast mixture in.
  3. Knead the dough about 7 to 10 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl or other container, cover it, and let it rise for 1 to 2 hours, whatever fits your schedule.
  5. ently deflate the dough, and divide it into four pieces, for medium-crust pizza; or three pieces, for thicker crusts. Use a rolling pin to roll each piece, on a lightly greased surface, into a circle to fit a 12" pizza pan. Let the dough rest several times to relax it and make it more cooperative. If you prefer a more artisan look to your crust, with scattered random air pockets throughout, hand-stretch the dough to size. Again, letting it rest periodically makes the job easier., Place the rounds on pizza pans; on baking sheets; or, if you have a pizza stone in your oven, on parchment.,
  6. Preheat your oven to 450°F.
  7. While it's heating, get out your toppings, which you've prepared ahead. Some possibilities include sliced pepperoni; sautéed mushrooms, onions, or peppers; cooked meats; olives; anchovies; and grated or shredded cheese., Brush each crust with a bit of olive oil; spread pizza or spaghetti sauce lightly over the surface, and let your guests add their favorite toppings. Sprinkle the top with grated cheese.,
  8. Bake the pizzas for 15 to 20 minutes, until they're golden brown, the toppings are hot and bubbly, and the cheese is melted. Remove the pizzas from the oven., Immediately transfer pizzas to a cooling rack, so their bottoms don't get soggy. After about 10 minutes (to allow the toppings to set), slice and serve; a pair of scissors is a great cutting tool.

Optional cooking methods

    If making the “Viral leftover pizza sandwich” devide dough into two for two large sandwiches or more for smaller sandwich sizes. Flour a surface and roll dough out so it's about 1/4in. thick, oil the surface and sprinkle the outer inch with cornmeal (to allow you to open it more easily). Fold in half and cook in preheated oven for ~15 minutes or until parts of the surface are golden brown.
